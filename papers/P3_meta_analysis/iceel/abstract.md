*Cultural dimensions in online learning: A Hofstede-moderated
meta-analysis of the Big Five and academic achievement, with a focused
synthesis of the Japanese context.*

**Background.** The parent meta-analysis (Research Square preprint, DOI
10.21203/rs.3.rs-9513298/v1) reports a region moderator with one
significant effect: Extraversion — achievement is more negative in Asian
than in non-Asian online-learning samples. The within-Asia heterogeneity
that produces this pattern remains untested.

**Aim.** Treat each East-Asian study as a country-level observation,
attach Hofstede cultural-dimension scores, and meta-regress the Big Five —
achievement correlations on each dimension. Conclude with a focused
synthesis of the two Japan-based primary-pool studies (A-25 Tokiwa 2025;
A-31 Rivers 2021) against the wider East-Asian backdrop.

**Methods.** Asian-subset random-effects pool per trait (REML + HKSJ).
Single-dimension meta-regression on Power Distance, Individualism,
Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-Term Orientation, and Indulgence. Country
aggregation when k > 1 within country. Narrative comparison of Japan
studies on instrument family (BFI-2-J vs TIPI-J), modality, and education
level.

**Results.** Asian Conscientiousness pooled r = {{r_C_asia}}
[{{ci_lo_C_asia}}, {{ci_hi_C_asia}}]. Hofstede regression: Extraversion
on Individualism slope = {{slope_E_IND}} (p = {{p_E_IND}}),
Neuroticism on Long-Term Orientation slope = {{slope_N_LTO}} (p =
{{p_N_LTO}}). Japan studies show Conscientiousness r ranging from
{{r_C_japan_lo}} to {{r_C_japan_hi}}, both with confidence intervals
crossing zero given small N.

**Implications.** Cultural-moderator evidence is suggestive but
underpowered. The Japan-specific analysis underscores instrument
heterogeneity (60-item vs 10-item BFI variants) as a major confound that
future syntheses cannot ignore. Practical implication for Japanese ed-tech
designers: trait-based personalisation should not be deployed at scale on
the current evidence base.

**Keywords**: meta-analysis, Big Five, online learning, Hofstede,
cultural dimensions, Japan, ICEEL.

                                                                                                             Despite the fact that life outcome predictions are common and often involved in
                                                                                                          high-stakes decisions, they have not been the focus of research about the life course (for
                                                                                                          a few exceptions, see ref. 10). This limited attention means that there is little scientific
                                                                                                          foundation for understanding the degree of accuracy in these predictions. Nor is there
                                                                                                          an understanding of the fundamental processes that determine the predictability of life
                                                                                                          outcomes, and whether these might be overcome with more data, better algorithms, and
                                                                                                          improved theory.

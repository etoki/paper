## Disclosure

The author discloses the following potential conflict of interest:

The author is the founder and representative of SUNBLAZE Co., Ltd. (Tokyo, Japan), which provides HEXACO-JP, a proprietary Japanese-language HEXACO-based personality assessment service. This affiliation is reported as a potential conflict of interest in the spirit of full transparency.

## Three qualifications

The following points qualify the disclosure:

1. **The present study does not use HEXACO-JP.** The HEXACO instrument used in the analysis is the HEXACO-PI-R Japanese 60-item adaptation published by Wakabayashi (2014, *Japanese Psychological Research*, 56(3), 211–223, https://doi.org/10.1111/jpr.12045), a separately validated and openly cited instrument that predates and is independent of HEXACO-JP.

2. **The manuscript does not reference, evaluate, market, recommend, or otherwise promote HEXACO-JP or any other SUNBLAZE product or service.** No company product is named or evaluated in the body of the manuscript. SUNBLAZE Co., Ltd. did not commission, fund, sponsor, or direct the research. The research received no SUNBLAZE funding, no external grant funding, and was conducted as an independent academic project at no monetary cost.

3. **The headline empirical findings do not differentially favor commercial HEXACO-based assessment products.** The pre-registered counterfactual analysis returns a null contrast for the personality-intervention pathway (ΔP_A = −0.0061, 95% CI [−0.0207, +0.0062]) and a positive contrast for the structural-intervention pathway (ΔP_C = +0.0349, 95% CI [+0.0264, +0.0435]); the substantive reframing repositions HEXACO 7-typology as a stratification variable rather than as an intervention or screening target. These findings, taken together, constrain rather than promote the person-level commercial application of HEXACO-based assessments.

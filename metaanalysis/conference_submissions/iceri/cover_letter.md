## Manuscript summary

The Big Five online-learning literature has been pooled at the trait level for over a decade, but the joint distribution of **education level (K-12 / undergraduate / graduate)** and **academic discipline (STEM / humanities / mixed)** has never been mapped. The author's parent meta-analysis (Research Square preprint, DOI 10.21203/rs.3.rs-9513298/v1) executes Region, Era, and Outcome Type as moderators but explicitly notes that education level was pre-registered yet not quantitatively executed because no level had k ≥ 10, and discipline was not pre-registered at all.

The present ICERI paper executes a **3 × 3 cross-tabulated meta-analytic interaction model** on the corpus's primary-pool studies, reporting per-cell pooled correlations (Fisher z + REML + HKSJ) where k ≥ 2 and qualitative descriptions where 0 < k < 2. The headline result is that **the available evidence is overwhelmingly clustered in the undergraduate × mixed-discipline cell** (the largest cell by both k and combined N), while the K-12 row and the graduate row are almost completely empty. Of the K-12 primary-pool studies (A-25 Tokiwa, A-26 Wang), neither contributes extractable r values; A-10 Boonyapison (Thailand high school) does have extractable r but is excluded from the primary pool on modality grounds (face-to-face). The paper therefore documents not what the field knows but **where the field's knowledge actually lives**, and concludes with three concrete coverage-gap recommendations for ICERI's research-priorities audience.

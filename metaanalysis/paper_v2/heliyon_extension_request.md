Dear Ms Singh,

Thank you for the major-revision decision on Manuscript HELIYON-D-26-
02879, "Big Five Personality Traits and Academic Achievement in Online
Learning Environments: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." I am
grateful to both reviewers, and especially to Reviewer 1, for the
detailed and constructive critique.

I would like to take up the kind offer in your decision letter to
discuss a plan for additional time. Reviewer 1 raises a substantive
methodological concern — that the pre-registered six-database
systematic search was not fully executed in the originally submitted
version — and offers two honorable paths forward: either (i) conduct
a proper systematic search using the pre-registered databases and
resubmit, or (ii) reframe the manuscript as a scoping review with
correspondingly modest claims.

I would like to pursue path (i) as far as the publicly accessible
databases allow, supplemented by a transparent and explicit
acknowledgement of which databases remained outside the search.
Specifically, my plan is:

1. Conduct full Boolean-strategy searches in PubMed/MEDLINE
   (E-utilities), ERIC, and DOAJ — all of which are open-access and
   do not require institutional credentials — as well as a structured
   Google Scholar search.

2. Disclose transparently that PsycINFO, Scopus, Web of Science, and
   ProQuest Dissertations remain inaccessible without institutional
   subscription, and report explicitly which databases were and were
   not searched.

3. Re-screen the resulting expanded candidate pool against the
   pre-registered eligibility criteria.

4. Re-extract and re-analyse with the existing pipeline.

5. Update the PRISMA flow diagram, the PRISMA 2020 checklist (Item 6
   in particular), the Methods section, and the Limitations section
   to match the actual executed search.

In parallel, I will address the remaining nineteen points raised by
Reviewer 1 (including reporting actual intra-rater kappa values,
clarifying the beta-to-r conversion procedure for Yu (2021),
reconciling the R-versus-Python statement between Methods and
Acknowledgments, distinguishing Cohen and Baruth (2017) from Baruth
and Cohen (2023) consistently, labeling the PASH-online extension as
the author's own proposal, and grounding the moderator interpretation
more carefully) and the seven points raised by Reviewer 2 (small-pool
implications, single-reviewer rationale, blended-versus-fully-online
clarification, beta-conversion caveats, publication-bias power,
cautious causal language, and a tighter practical-implications
subsection).

Given the scope of the additional search, re-screening, re-extraction,
and re-analysis required to address Reviewer 1's central concern in
the manner the reviewer requested, I would be very grateful for an
extension of the revision deadline from 06 July 2026 to approximately
31 August 2026 (eight additional weeks).

If a shorter extension would be preferable to the editorial office, I
am happy to discuss a more compressed timeline; if a longer one is
acceptable, I would use it to ensure the revised search and analysis
are fully transparent and reproducible. Either way, I am committed to
returning a substantially strengthened manuscript that responds to
every point raised by both reviewers.

Thank you for considering this request, and please let me know if any
additional information would help with your decision.

## What was wrong

In the v2 reference list, my own previously published primary study (study A-25 in the corpus) was listed as:

> Tokiwa, E. (2025). Big Five personality traits and online learning outcomes in Japanese high school students [Manuscript in preparation]. SUNBLAZE Co., Ltd.

This is incorrect. The actual citation is a published, open-access (CC BY) article in *Frontiers in Psychology*:

> **Tokiwa, E. (2025). Who excels in online learning in Japan? *Frontiers in Psychology, 16*, Article 1420996. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1420996** (received 21 April 2024, accepted 28 February 2025, published 5 May 2025).

The error was a stale placeholder that originated in an early draft and propagated through to the v2 reference list despite the end-to-end audit reported in the v2 cover letter (item 5: bibliographic fields cross-validated against Crossref). I take full responsibility for the oversight; the article was indexed in Crossref and verifiable, and the placeholder should have been caught during the audit pass.

## Scope: results are unchanged

This correction is confined to the **reference list and to the prose where the same study is cited inline as the conflict-of-interest study**. **No quantitative result, table, figure, sensitivity analysis, or GRADE assessment changes**, for the following pre-registered reason: study A-25 (Tokiwa, 2025) reported test-completion outcomes without extractable trait-by-achievement zero-order Pearson correlations, and therefore did not contribute effect sizes to the primary quantitative pool. The pre-registered "exclude COI study" sensitivity analysis already showed |Δr| < .001 for every Big Five trait in the v1/v2 results, and that remains true in the corrected version.
